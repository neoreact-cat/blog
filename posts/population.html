Population of Russia

The First Chapter

January 21, 2018 - 3 minute read -

Russia is a complex and inequal state.
Russian regions vary in size, wealth, population, autonomy, etc.

This chapter is all about population.

All regions are united for eight federal districts.
Each of them has a huge territory and population as shown at the following map:

Map #1. Russian population by federal districts (Click To Open A Full Size)

Russia’s population is more than 146 million.
The largest number of people live in the Central district.
Far Eastern district has the smallest population.

Table #1. Russian population by federal districts (Click To Open A Full Size)

Let’s look more detailed. There are 85 regions in Russia:

Map #2. Russian population by regions (Click To Open A Full Size)

The Central Federal District is very centralized and high-concentrated around Moscow.
19,4 millions of people live in Moscow and Moscow oblast (12,7% of Russia’s population)
Another leaders is Krasnodarsky krai, Saint Petersburg, Sverdlovsk and Rostov oblast.
Several regions in South, Volga, Ural, Central and Siberian federal districts also high-populated.

Far East, Far North and some regions in Siberia are not.
